Hello! I'm Zac. I'm currently a postdoctoral scholar in the Rennison Lab at University of California - San Diego and a Lecturer at the University of San Diego. I am a broadly trained biologist with particular interest in understanding how organisms, particularly those in aquatic habitats, rapidly adapt to novel environmental and ecological conditions. Over the years I have investigated this question and related themes in a variety of study systems, ranging from tiny estuarine crabs and their barnacle parasite, a globally invasive marine tunicate, and the inimitable threespine stickleback. My work takes a holistic perspective by integrateing across levels of biological organization -- from molecular pathways to organismal function to ecosystem-level processes. This approach gives me the fantastic opportunity conduct fieldwork in breathtaking locations, perform molecular magic in the laboratory, and make sense of oodles of sequence data at the helm of a supercomputer.
