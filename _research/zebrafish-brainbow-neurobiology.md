My earliest sustained research experience focused on development of the larval zebrafish hindbrain using Brainbow and fluorescence microscopy. By blending expression of differently colored fluorescent proteins, Brainbow makes neighboring cells visually distinct and allows related cells to retain a shared color through multiple divisions.

I helped build transgenic tools, maintain zebrafish lines, perform microinjections and molecular cloning, and collect confocal time-lapse images. These approaches allowed us to follow neural progenitors and their descendants *in vivo*, revealing patterns of cell division, migration, and coordinated cell death during development.

This work also included collaborations using zebrafish to investigate alpha-synuclein biology and Parkinson's disease.
